Introduction

Over the last two years, there has been a great deal of increase in digital transformation. This has been a trend for some time now, but the need for digitalisation was most evident at the outbreak of the pandemic. Many companies have had to find new ways of working, and for most companies, ERP systems form the core of their business-critical tools.

Whether or not an ERP system is a necessity for the modern day business, is no longer in question. Especially for Distributors, Wholesalers, Merchants and any type of Distributive traders. Many businesses, however, often implement their new systems and then get ‘comfortable’ as the years go on. The need to upgrade to the latest versions usually don’t get much attention. Business either think it’s not necessary or they might just not want to go through the hassle.

Why?

There are has many risks associated with staying on old and out dated software versions. The flip side of that, is that in this day and age it’s very important to get current and to stay current, which means your business will be using the latest technology and could be better prepared for unforeseen circumstances or even just to keep up with modern day customer expectations.

Benefits?

  • Improved Support Levels
  • Reductions in bugs
  • Access to key features or mobile technology like e-apps
  • Better functionality
  • Stable platform to continue on your upgrade path
